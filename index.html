About the Agricultural Training Institute

Mission, Vision, and Institutional Commitment

The Agricultural Training Institute serves as the capacity-building arm of the Department of Agriculture, equipping extension workers, farmers, fisherfolk, and partner institutions with relevant knowledge, responsive services, and learning opportunities.

This HRIS portal supports ATI personnel in delivering timely workforce services while aligning daily operations with institutional priorities, training excellence, and public accountability.

Mission

Empowering stakeholders through a resilient and responsive agriculture and fisheries extension system.

Vision

A competitive and prosperous agriculture and fisheries sector towards a vibrant economy.

Training Programs

Featured ATI institutional activities and extension learning programs.

Flagship Course

Agricultural Development Officers of the Community (AgRiDOC)

A season-long experiential course founded on transformational leadership and implemented through the IPAD project to develop a new breed of rice extension professionals.

Quality Assurance

Specialist Training on Participatory Guarantee System (PGS)

An ATI course that supports quality assurance for organic products while strengthening farmer participation, verification, decision-making, and market engagement.

Training of Trainers

Season-Long Training of Trainers (SL-TOT) for HVCDP

A rigorous crop-production course for banner program focal and technical staff who will later cascade technologies through farmer field schools in their respective regions.
